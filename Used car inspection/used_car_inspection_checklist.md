# Used Car Inspection Checklist (Step-by-Step)

## Stage 1: Before Starting the Car (Cold Inspection)
- Start with a **cold engine**.
- Use a **torch/flashlight**.

### Engine Bay Checks
- Check **battery manufacturing date**.
- Check **engine oil level** using dipstick.
- Inspect for **fluid leaks** (radiator cap, pipes, clamps, engine oil).
- Look for **shiny/new parts** (recent replacements).
- Check for **drilled holes** (modifications).
- Inspect **engine belt condition**.
- Check **alternator oxidation** (should match engine).
- Look for **rodent bite marks** on wires/pipes.
- Inspect **weld/hammer marks** on chassis rails (accident signs).
- Check **air filter condition**.

## Stage 2: Exterior Inspection
- Check **panel gaps and bumper alignment**.
- Inspect **paint consistency**.
- Check for **rust** (door seals, boot).
- Look for **condensation** in headlights/tail lights.
- Verify **all wheel nuts** are present.
- Check **window glass markings** (should match).
- Inspect **spare tyre condition**.

## Stage 3: Tyres & Suspension
- Check **tyre age and tread depth**.
- Inspect **suspension** for rust/leaks.
- Check for **play in tyres**.
- Spin tyres for **noise or resistance**.

## Stage 4: Interior & Electricals
- Check **infotainment and speakers**.
- Test **horn** (dimming lights = weak battery).
- Test **door locks, windows, ORVMs** repeatedly.
- Check **wipers** (smooth movement, proper return).
- Check **cabin air filter**.

## Stage 5: Functional Checks
- Run car with **AC ON for 10+ minutes**.

## Stage 6: Test Drive
- Drive **without music**.
- Full steering lock both sides (check noise/stiffness).
- Drive over bumps (listen for suspension noise).
- Hard brake test (check bite/stability).
- Release steering briefly (car should go straight).
- Check wind noise (sunroof seal condition).

## Stage 7: Advanced Checks
- Check **brake fluid**.
- Inspect **spark plugs** (clean vs carbon deposits).
- Inspect **throttle body** (should be clean).

## Stage 8: Exhaust Check
- Check exhaust for **splutter, leaks, or smoke**.

## Pro Tips
- Inspect systematically (left to right).
- Check service history.
- Consider a mechanic inspection before purchase.
